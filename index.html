Ariana Grande - Positions (34+35)

This album contains a variety of catchy songs. Ranging from catchy upbeat tunes to ones you want to slow dance in the rain with. Recorded during the COVID-19 Pandemic, she stated that the focus of this album was her emotional healing. Positions was 2021's eighth-biggest album on the Billboard 200. The title track, "Positions", accompanied by its music video, was released on October 23, 2020, as the album's lead single. Grande takes you on a ride with the different emotions that one will definitely relate to when they listen to this album. Our personal favorite? 34+35. We feel that this song perfectly encapsulates everything we love about the pop singer. It's catchy, fun, and incorporates some great vocals.

BTS - Love Yourself: Tear (Paradise)

Love Yourself: 轉 ‘Tear’, which follows the 2017 mini album Love Yourself: ‘Her’, is a kaleidoscopic mark of that efficiency, observing the finely tuned formula BTS have been perfecting since 2015. ‘Tear’, like ‘Her’, is a concept album of sorts. Roughly half the songs adhere to the album’s subhead. If ‘Her’ was an assortment of heart-professing love songs, then ‘Tear’ is the inverse. It deals with the cycle of grief that lingers through a separation. In Paradise, they're telling us that we don't need to beat ourselves up over not achieving anything great right away, because life is long and we have so much time to accomplish what we want. We're all aiming for the same finish line: "a paradise."

Our Rating:

Ed Sheeran - X (Thinking Out Loud)

As the title implies, “x” (pronounced “multiply”), Sheeran’s highly anticipated follow-up, ups the ante from his debut. He sinks even deeper into feelings of love, jealousy and inebriation while trying to navigate pop superstardom — a problem this album is sure to only amplify. "Thinking out loud" serves as a heartfelt and romantic addition to the dreamy heartthrob's album. Getting married anytime soon? This would be a lovely choice for you and your lover to slow dance to. However, the album ultimately falls short as it essentially presents more of the same that Sheeran has produced over the years. The lack of creativity, repetitive nature and overly sappy feel earns itself a lower rating.
